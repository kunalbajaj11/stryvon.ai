DemandPulse

AI-powered demand forecasting and inventory intelligence for industrial distribution.

Stock smarter, reduce dead inventory, improve service levels.

PVF Inventory Multi-Warehouse Planning Project Demand Readiness Working Capital Control

Business Challenge

  • Excess inventory accumulates in slow-moving material grades, pressure classes, and legacy spec combinations.
  • Fast-moving project materials face frequent shortages during EPC bid conversion periods.
  • Long vendor lead times and mill schedules increase planning risk for critical items.
  • Limited branch-to-branch visibility causes duplicate buying while other warehouses hold transferable stock.
  • Demand varies sharply by region, sector, and customer type across oil & gas, power, and industrial accounts.
  • Manual planning cycles struggle to react to demand uncertainty in long-cycle B2B supply operations.

Product Concept

Demand forecasting by operational dimension

Forecasts by SKU, product family, branch, region, and customer segment to support procurement and allocation planning.

Reorder timing prediction

Projects reorder windows based on demand velocity, lead-time behavior, and service-level targets.

Warehouse transfer recommendations

Suggests stock transfers to rebalance inventory before new purchase orders are triggered.

Dead-stock and slow-mover flags

Highlights items with aging risk and proposes branch/channel liquidation options.

Project-impact simulation

Simulates stock impact from new project wins or major RFQ conversions across time horizons.

Shortage risk intelligence

Identifies critical materials likely to stock out before committed delivery dates.

Core Modules

Demand forecasting engine

Combines seasonality, project patterns, and historical order behavior.

Reorder recommendation engine

Generates suggested buy dates and quantities with confidence bands.

Safety stock optimizer

Balances service reliability against working-capital constraints.

Branch balancing / stock transfer suggestions

Reduces excess purchases through transfer-aware planning.

Inventory aging dashboard

Tracks dead stock and liquidation risk by material family and location.

Supplier lead-time intelligence

Monitors variability and late-risk trends by vendor and commodity.

Scenario planning module

Tests effects of major project wins, demand spikes, and delays.

Sales pipeline

Opportunity stages and expected close windows for project-driven demand.

Example Workflows

Reorder planning: system proposes reorder date and quantity per SKU based on target service level, lead-time uncertainty, and demand forecast confidence.
Inter-warehouse transfer recommendation: identifies surplus in one branch and shortage in another, with transfer vs buy cost comparison.
Shortage prediction before project delivery: flags potential material gaps before committed customer delivery milestones.
Dead stock liquidation insight: surfaces slow movers and recommends discounting, cross-branch redeployment, or alternate customer targeting.

Implementation Plan

1Phase 1: Dashboards and Stock Visibility

  • Unify inventory and movement visibility across warehouses.
  • Launch baseline KPIs for aging, stockouts, and service levels.
  • Create branch/region executive scorecards.

2Phase 2: Forecast Engine

  • Deploy SKU/category forecast models with confidence intervals.
  • Tune by region, customer segment, and project pattern.
  • Benchmark forecast quality against existing planning method.

3Phase 3: Recommendations and Automation

  • Enable reorder and transfer recommendation workflows.
  • Integrate approval controls and exception handling.
  • Operationalize continuous learning from outcomes.

Why This Fits a Global PVF Supplier

Designed for high-value, long-cycle material categories

DemandPulse is built for industrial product structures where procurement lead times, minimum buys, and grade/class variants materially affect capital and service performance.

Optimized for multi-branch distribution networks

The platform supports balancing decisions across regional warehouses, reducing duplicate purchases and improving network-level stock utilization.

Handles varied technical grades and demand volatility

It accounts for differences across carbon steel, stainless, alloy, valve classes, and project-driven demand spikes that generic retail planning tools miss.

Aligns inventory with B2B service commitments

Recommendations prioritize customer delivery reliability and commercial commitments for large industrial accounts, not just generic turns optimization.
