DocTrace

Automated quality and compliance documentation for industrial material supply.

Turn complex documentation into a competitive advantage.

MTC & Compliance Automation Project Documentation Control Traceability by Heat/Batch Audit-Ready Operations

Problem Statement

  • Material test certificates, inspection reports, and vendor documentation are often scattered across email threads, folders, and branch systems.
  • Teams spend disproportionate time chasing suppliers for missing quality and compliance documents.
  • Manual validation of heat numbers, grades, and standards causes avoidable delays in order release and project closeout.
  • Customers expect quick, accurate document packs for acceptance, QA records, and regulatory handover.
  • Limited traceability across order lines, project deliveries, and supplier records creates execution and credibility risk.
  • Without structured control, documentation quality becomes a bottleneck in high-value industrial deliveries.

Product Overview

Central industrial documentation repository

Stores MTCs, inspection reports, vendor declarations, and delivery records in one controlled platform.

Deep operational tagging

Tags by order, project, SKU, heat number, batch, supplier, and customer for precise retrieval.

Validation workflows

Runs rule-based checks for mandatory fields, standards compliance, and document completeness.

Missing-document alerts

Flags pending documentation gaps before dispatch or customer handover milestones.

Customer-ready document packs

Builds structured, branded packs aligned to project and contractual documentation requirements.

Audit-ready traceability

Maintains immutable history from supplier upload through internal approval and customer release.

Workflow

1. Supplier uploads docs: certificates and inspection files arrive via portal, email, or structured API feed.
2. System tags and validates: OCR/metadata extraction links each file to order, heat, batch, and supplier context.
3. Internal reviewer approves: QA/QC confirms technical compliance, completeness, and release readiness.
4. Customer pack generated: approved files are compiled into project-specific handover packages.
5. Full traceability retained: every change, approval, and dispatch action is logged for audit defense.

Implementation Phases

1Phase 1: Document Repository and Tagging

  • Launch centralized industrial documentation repository.
  • Enable metadata tagging by order, SKU, supplier, heat, and batch.
  • Establish baseline dashboards for missing and pending records.

2Phase 2: Validation and Approval Workflows

  • Implement compliance checklist rules by project and customer type.
  • Configure QA/QC approval routing with SLA and escalation controls.
  • Activate missing-document alerting and supplier follow-up automation.

3Phase 3: Customer Packs and Compliance Analytics

  • Automate customer-ready documentation pack generation.
  • Introduce supplier compliance scoring and audit readiness analytics.
  • Operationalize continuous improvement through compliance trend review.
