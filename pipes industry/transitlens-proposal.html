TransitLens

Real-time shipment visibility and delivery risk intelligence for industrial supply chains.

Move from reactive tracking to proactive delivery assurance.

Control Tower Visibility ETA Risk Intelligence Project Delivery Assurance Customer Confidence

Business Pain Points

  • Limited end-to-end shipment visibility across long-distance and multi-leg industrial deliveries.
  • Updates remain fragmented across freight forwarders, transporters, and regional logistics partners.
  • Delay communication often arrives too late for meaningful mitigation or client expectation reset.
  • Customer escalations increase when project teams cannot get reliable delivery confidence windows.
  • Branches and account teams lack a single control tower view for committed delivery dates.
  • Project-critical shipments for shutdowns and turnarounds require tighter operational certainty than basic tracking provides.

Sample Workflow

1. Order released: customer order and project commitment are registered against delivery SLA targets.
2. Shipment booked: booking details from freight partner are ingested and linked to order/project records.
3. Milestones updated: transit events and documentation states refresh the control tower in near real-time.
4. Delay detected: ETA model identifies variance beyond commitment threshold.
5. Internal team alerted: logistics and account owners receive exception notifications with risk context.
6. Customer notified proactively: structured update is sent with revised ETA and mitigation status.
7. Escalation logged: mitigation actions and outcomes are captured for governance and future analytics.

Implementation Phases

1Phase 1: Order-to-Shipment Visibility

  • Connect order, PO, and shipment records into a unified control tower.
  • Surface baseline milestone tracking and branch-level views.
  • Establish SLA definitions and visibility standards.

2Phase 2: Risk Alerts and ETA Intelligence

  • Deploy delay-risk logic and confidence-based ETA calculations.
  • Route exceptions to responsible internal owners.
  • Build root-cause analytics across carriers and lanes.

3Phase 3: Customer-Facing Delivery Updates

  • Enable automated client notifications and portal updates.
  • Introduce escalation and mitigation logging for governance.
  • Operationalize account-level delivery assurance reporting.

Why This Fits Industrial PVF Distribution

Project-critical delivery windows demand certainty

Industrial PVF orders frequently support shutdowns, turnarounds, and construction milestones where late material delivery can trigger cascading operational and contractual impact.

Clients value predictability as much as price

In large B2B material supply, reliable commitments and transparent risk communication are strategic differentiators. TransitLens helps distributors deliver certainty, not just shipment status updates.
