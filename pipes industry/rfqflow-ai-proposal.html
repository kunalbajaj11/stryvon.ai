RFQFlow AI

AI-powered RFQ automation for industrial distributors and PVF supply businesses.

Reduce quote turnaround time, improve pricing accuracy, and standardize bid workflows.

Pipes Valves Fittings Flanges EPC / Oil & Gas / Power

Industry Problem

  • Manual RFQ parsing from PDFs, emails, BOQs, and spreadsheet attachments creates bottlenecks during high-volume bid cycles.
  • Cross-checking line-item specs such as size, grade, schedule, end connection, and standard references is slow and error-prone.
  • Quote quality and response speed often depend on individual coordinators and estimators rather than a repeatable process.
  • Vendor comparison is inconsistent when quote formats, lead times, and technical alternates are not normalized.
  • Pricing logic can vary by team member, creating avoidable margin leakage on complex bids.
  • Large EPC and oil & gas customers expect rapid, technically accurate responses that are difficult to deliver at scale.

Product Overview

Multi-format intake

Upload RFQ PDF, Excel sheet, BOQ export, or email attachments into one standardized intake queue.

AI line extraction

Extracts item description, NPS size, schedule, material grade, class/rating, quantity, and standards from unstructured documents.

Catalog mapping

Suggests internal SKU candidates and closest catalog equivalents for valves, fittings, flanges, and pipe materials.

Vendor routing

Routes line items to approved vendors based on commodity, geography, framework terms, and historical service levels.

Normalized comparison

Builds a like-for-like supplier quote matrix with technical compliance checks, lead times, and price variance views.

Quote orchestration

Generates customer quotation drafts, manages revisions, and tracks approval flow from coordinator to management sign-off.

Sample Workflow

1. RFQ received - Email inbox, customer portal, or manual upload initiates a controlled intake record.
2. Parsed by system - OCR and AI extraction classify each line item and technical attribute.
3. Item mapping - Items are mapped to internal product master and approved vendor set.
4. Quote comparison generated - Supplier submissions are normalized into a comparable matrix.
5. Sales team review - Commercial pricing and margin checks are completed with guardrails.
6. Approval - Workflow routes for financial or managerial approval based on thresholds.
7. Quotation sent - Final proposal is issued to customer with full revision traceability.
8. Win/loss tracked - Outcomes feed analytics for vendor strategy and pricing performance.

Implementation Phases

1Phase 1: RFQ Intake + Parsing

  • Ingest email and portal RFQs into a unified queue.
  • Deploy OCR and extraction workflows for line-item structuring.
  • Establish baseline confidence scoring and manual review checkpoints.

2Phase 2: Quote Comparison + Pricing Engine

  • Introduce normalized supplier quote matrix and alternative mapping.
  • Apply rule-based pricing support with margin guardrails.
  • Enable structured approval routing for exceptions.

3Phase 3: ERP Integration + Analytics

  • Connect to ERP and inventory systems for commercial integrity.
  • Launch executive dashboards for turnaround, win/loss, and margin trends.
  • Operationalize continuous improvement with closed-loop reporting.
